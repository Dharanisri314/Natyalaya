INTRODUCTION

Embark on a breathtaking journey through the world of Kuchipudi with the renowned Sreelakshmy Govardhanan. In this immersive Indian classical dance lecture series, teacher will unravel the intricacies of a special Javali by Carnatic legend Mysore Sadashiva Rao titled Ekkadunnavu Naa Chakkani Swami. It empowers the student to delve into this timeless piece's intricate choreography, rhythmic complexities, and dynamic storytelling. With Sreelakshmy's expertise and passion, you'll be transported into the heart of Carnatic tradition, offering a unique and profound understanding of the dance form and the literary genius of Sadashiva Rao. Sadashiva Rao served the famous Wodeyar clan of Mysore and belongs to the line of Carnatic icon Tyagaraja. The heroine implores her beloved to shake from his stupor and attend to her in this composition. Javali's are fast-paced productions focusing on the shringara rasa, and the Guru beautifully showcases our nayikas emotionally charged journey. These classical dance sessions celebrate Kuchipudi's splendour, where artistry, history, and cultural depth converge.

ABOUT THE COMPOSITION

The nayika awaits her royal patron, King Krishnaraja Wodeyar of Mysore. She seeks his protection from the pesky Kamadeva, simultaneously chiding him yet showering him with praises. This declaration refers to the feelings of love and desire growing within her. In the Charanams, she ups the ante by referring to how even sages succumb to the temptations of passion, and hence, a mere mortal like her stands no chance.

ABOUT THE CLASS

The Guru has spaced this course with a Pallavi and three Charanams. A general introduction and a detailed meaning of the javali is also explained. Javali’s focus is on the abhinaya or expressions, where the viewer is gently guided throughout the presentation. The Guru provides exhaustive insight on how to perform the same during the lectures.
